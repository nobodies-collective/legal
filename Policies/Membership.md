# NCA Membership / Collaboratorship policy

1. Membership (_Asociado_). Any person can apply for **membership** in the Association. Membership can be granted when the applicant:
    1. Has attended at least one Event (defined as a Nowhere or NCA event within the 3 years preceding their application); 
    2. Is not disqualified under the Association’s statutes or policies; AND
    3. Agrees to the Membership Agreement, and:
    4. Satisfies at least one of the following
        1.  Was an Original Elector in the January 2026 Nobodies Collective Board (Junta Directiva) election; OR
        2.  Has served as a “Norg Metalead”, “Norg Lead”, “Barrio Lead” at at least 1 Event (such leadership counting upon satisfactory completion of that Event); OR
        3. Has served as a “Norg Volunteer” at an Event on 2 or more occasions AND has contributed to at least 1 other burn event in a meaningful coordination, leadership, operational, or expert capacity; OR
        4. Has attended an Event 3 or more times AND has served as a Lead at another burn event.
        5. Has served in a defined coordination, leadership, governance, or expert contributor role for the Association or a Barrio for a minimum of three consecutive months in preparation for or support of an upcoming Event.

2. Collaboratorship (_Colaborador_): 
    1. Any person can apply for **collaboratorship** in the Association. Collaboratorship can be granted when the applicant:
        1. Is not disqualified under the Association’s statutes or policies; AND 
        2. Agrees to the Collaboratorship Agreement.
    2. Collaborators are not members (Asociados) of the Association and do not hold voting rights or other governance rights under the Statutes.
    3. Collaborators may apply for membership where they subsequently meet the eligibility criteria set out in this policy.

3. Approval & Rejection. 
    1. Membership and Collaboratorship are granted by majority vote of the Board. 
    2. An eligible application may be denied where a safeguarding concern has been identified by the Participant Wellness or R&R teams.
    3. The board reserves the right to change these policies from time to time to maintain their alignment with the Association’s organizational values and legal compliance.

4. Policy Evolution.
    1. The Board will encourage the creation of a community-developed membership policy to be put to a vote at the first post-event General Assembly.
