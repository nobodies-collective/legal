# NOBODIES COLLECTIVE ASSOCIATION

---

# STATUTES

\*In the event of any discrepancy between the Spanish version and the English version, the Spanish version shall prevail.

---

## CHAPTER I. NAME, PURPOSES AND REGISTERED OFFICE

### Article 1. Name, Legal Capacity and Duration, Registered Office and Territorial Scope.

1. Under the name "Asociación Nobodies Collective" (hereinafter "Nobodies" or the "Association"), it is constituted as a cultural, non-profit organization and conducts its activities under the protection of Article 22 of the Spanish Constitution and shall be governed in accordance with the provisions of Organic Law 1/2002, of 22 March, regulating the right of association, and the general rules and concordant provisions that may be applicable at any time, as well as by the current Statutes.

2. The Association shall have its own legal personality, separate from that of its members and associates, and full legal capacity to act, in accordance with these Statutes and, in general, with the provisions of the legislation in force on associations.

   The Association may collaborate with or become part of other associations, organizations, bodies, public and private institutions at national and international level, provided that their purposes are common and/or contribute to the better development of the principles and/or purposes of the organization.

   This Association is established for an indefinite period of time.

3. The Association establishes its registered office at Glorieta de Bilbao 1, 3º Derecha, 28004, Madrid.

   Its territorial scope for its activities covers the whole of Spanish territory.

   Any change of registered office shall require a resolution of the General Assembly, convened specifically for this purpose, with the corresponding amendment of these Statutes.

   The resolution of the General Assembly must be notified to the Register of Associations within one month and shall only take effect, both for members and third parties, from the date of its registration.

### Article 2. Purposes of the Association.

1. The Association, as an entity of national scope, shall have, among others, the following purposes:

   a. To foster and respect the ten fundamental principles on which Nobodies is based:

      1. Community.

      2. Cooperation.

      3. Gifting.

      4. Immediacy.

      5. Inclusion.

      6. Leave no trace.

      7. No commerce.

      8. Participation.

      9. Self-expression.

      10. Self-reliance.

   b. To ensure respect for Consent of any person participating in any of the activities of the Association.

   c. To act as a channel of opinion and spokesperson for all its members before public administrations and other public or private institutions and bodies, both at state and regional level, as well as before European bodies, in relation to the purposes and scope of action of the Association.

   d. To promote collaboration, cooperation, representation and participation of the Association in other associations, business organizations, public or private bodies or institutions, at regional, national or European level with a similar object.

2. The coverage area of the Association shall be Spanish territory.

### Article 3. Activities of the Association.

The activities that the Association shall implement in order to fulfil its purposes are:

1. The organization of a cultural and participative event with an annual focus, in accordance with the fundamental principles.

2. The organization of events of a cultural, educational, artistic, environmental, sporting or any other nature that are aligned with the purposes and principles of the Association.

3. Collaboration with entities of any nature, insofar as the principles are respected and the achievement of the purposes of the Association is assisted.

4. Acquisition of ownership or enjoyment of real estate for the better achievement of the purposes of the Association.

5. Any other action that leads to the better achievement of its purposes.

## CHAPTER II. GOVERNING BODIES AND REPRESENTATION OF THE ASSOCIATION. THE MEMBERS OF THE ASSOCIATION, THEIR RIGHTS AND OBLIGATIONS.

### Article 4. Governing and representative bodies.

The governing and representative bodies of the Association are, respectively:

- General Assembly of the Association.

- Board of Trustees.

---

### Section One

### On the General Assembly

### Article 5. Composition and Structure of the General Assembly.

1. The General Assembly is the supreme body of the Association. It is made up of all the members belonging to it, and its decisions are binding on all members, including those absent, dissenting or incapacitated.

2. Nobodies is established as an Association of persons equal in rights and duties, who freely adhere to the Association. All members constitute the General Assembly of Nobodies, which is the sovereign body and, ultimately, the legitimate representative before the authorities in relation to Nobodies.

3. Likewise, a smaller Board of Trustees shall be established from among the members of the Assembly, in order to carry out the executive tasks of Nobodies and the administrative tasks inherent to it, and it must be accountable for and have its actions endorsed by the General Assembly.

4. The Board of Trustees shall promote and collaborate, as far as possible, with different specific Working Groups for the development of its purposes.

### Article 6. Members and Participation.

1. The General Assembly is the sovereign body of Nobodies; the members of the Association form part of it by their own inalienable right.

2. The members of Nobodies, meeting in a duly convened General Assembly, decide by majority on matters within its competence.

3. All members are subject to the resolutions of the General Assembly, including those absent, those who disagree and those present who have abstained from voting.

### Article 7. Powers and Authorities of the General Assembly.

The General Assembly has the power to:

1. Amend these Statutes of the Association. For this purpose, the presence of half of the members and a qualified majority of 2/3 of the votes of those present in favour shall be required.

2. Elect the members of the governing body (Board of Trustees) and supervise its activity.

3. Approve the annual budget and the annual financial statements, as well as adopt resolutions regarding the form and amount of the contribution to the support of Nobodies and approve the management carried out by the governing body.

4. Resolve, where appropriate, on the dissolution of the Association.

5. Join, where appropriate, other Associations, or withdraw from them.

6. Request the declaration of public utility from the competent authorities, in any field (European, national, regional, municipal) where it may be required.

7. Approve by majority vote, endorse, observe and ultimately ensure compliance with the Internal Regulations.

8. Decide on the removal or definitive separation, after the appropriate proceedings, of members, in accordance with the provisions in Article 25.

9. Decide on any other matter not directly attributed to any other body within Nobodies.

### Article 8. Call, Holding and Documentation of General Assembly Meetings.

1. The General Assembly shall meet in ordinary session at least once a year.

2. The governing body may convene the General Assembly on an extraordinary basis whenever it deems it appropriate, and must do so when requested by, at least, half of the members; in this case, the Assembly shall take place within thirty days from the request.

   Such Assembly may be held either in person or by telematic means. Likewise, the use of electronic or remote voting methods shall be permitted in all cases for the adoption of resolutions, provided that no participant expressly objects to this system and that the identity of the members and the validity of the votes cast are duly guaranteed.

3. The Assembly shall be convened by the Board of Trustees by means of a notice that shall contain, at least, the agenda, the place (which may be online, in which case the access instructions shall be provided), date and time of the meeting.

4. The call must be communicated 15 days before the date of the meeting, individually and by email addressed to all members. In the case of an extraordinary call, it shall be governed in accordance with the provisions set out in Article 10.

5. Meetings of the General Assembly shall be chaired by the President of the Board of Trustees; in his or her absence, by the Vice President; and in the absence of both, by the member of the Board of Trustees designated by the Assembly.

   Without prejudice to the foregoing, the General Assembly may appoint, at the beginning of each session, a moderator elected from among its members, who shall be responsible for managing speaking turns and conducting the debate.

6. The Secretary of the Board of Trustees shall draw up the minutes of each meeting, which must be signed by him or her and by the President of the Assembly, including a summary of the deliberations, the text of the resolutions adopted, the numerical result of the votes and the list of attendees.

7. The minutes must be sent within the month following the meeting. Once reviewed and approved by the attendees, their final version may be validated by electronic means.

8. At the beginning of each meeting of the General Assembly, the Secretary shall read the minutes of the previous session. It shall also be the responsibility of the Secretary to send, at least fifteen days in advance, the corresponding minutes and any other documentation to the representatives of the General Assembly.

### Article 9. Quorum and Agenda.

1. The General Assembly (whether ordinary or extraordinary) shall be validly constituted on first convocation when at least half plus one of its members, present or represented, attend, without numerical limit. On second convocation, the Assembly shall be validly constituted with the members with voting rights present or represented therein, regardless of the number of attendees. The agenda for the meetings shall state the first and second convocation.

2. A request for the additional inclusion on the agenda of one or more items to be discussed may be made by any member, provided that at least 20% of the members take part in said request. If the Assembly has already been convened, such request may be submitted to the Board of Trustees up to 15 calendar days before the date on which this body is to meet. The Assembly may only adopt resolutions on the items included on the agenda, unless it has been constituted as a universal meeting or the resolutions refer to the convening of a new General Assembly. Items that have been discussed but not included on the agenda must be included —and, where appropriate, voted on and endorsed— at the next Assembly, whether ordinary or extraordinary.

### Article 10. Voting and Adoption of Resolutions of the General Assembly.

1. At meetings of the General Assembly, each member is entitled to one vote.

2. Resolutions are adopted by simple majority (more votes in favour than against). In the event of a tie between opposing options —and only in this case, and not applicable to the election of positions on the Board of Trustees— a casting vote may be exercised by the President of the Board of Trustees or, in his or her absence, by whoever chairs the Assembly.

3. Any member may delegate his or her vote to another member attending the Assembly, provided that such delegation has been notified by both parties to the Board of Trustees at least 24 hours prior to the Assembly.

4. Any amendment to these Statutes must be endorsed by the General Assembly, validly constituted with the attendance of at least half of the members, by the affirmative vote of a qualified majority of 2/3 of the members present.

5. To adopt resolutions on the dissolution of Nobodies, and on the disposal and transfer of its assets, the number of votes established in Article 32 shall be required.

6. Representation before the Ministry of the Interior (in accordance with Organic Law 1/2002, of 22 March, regulating the Right of Association) for the purposes of notifying any amendment to the Statutes of Nobodies, changes in the members of the Board of Trustees, or the possible dissolution of the Association, corresponds to the Secretary of the Board of Trustees, or, alternatively, to any other member of the Board expressly appointed by the Board for this purpose, and only under the relevant mandate from the General Assembly.

### Section Two

### Of the Board of Trustees

### Article 11. Composition and Election of the Board of Trustees.

1. The Board of Trustees is the representative body that manages and represents the interests of the Association in accordance with the provisions and directives of the General Assembly. This body is composed of:

   a. the President,

   b. the Vice President,

   c. the Secretary,

   d. the Treasurer

   e. up to nine (9) Non-Executive Members (Vocales).

2. The election of the members of the Board of Trustees shall be carried out by vote of the General Assembly, following the system established by the Internal Regulations. The persons elected shall take office after having accepted their position, as well as a Confidentiality Commitment in accordance with REGULATION (EU) 2016/679 OF THE EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT AND OF THE COUNCIL of 27 April 2016 on the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data and on the free movement of such data. The qualified majority for the designation of roles and confirmation of the elected positions shall be a simple majority (more votes in favour than against, explicitly excluding in this case the use of the casting vote by the current President in the event of a tie) as provided in Article 10.2.

3. The Nobodies Board shall be elected through elections every two years. In the event of the resignation or removal of a member of the Board of Trustees —except for the Presidency—, if the period until the next election call is less than 180 calendar days, their duties shall be assumed by the remaining members of the Board or delegated to one of those members designated by the President. If such resignation or removal occurs more than 180 calendar days before the next elections, the position must be subjected again to elections, for which an Extraordinary General Assembly shall be convened with that vote as the sole item on the agenda, to be called within no more than 60 calendar days and no less than 45.

4. The elections referred to in Article 11.3 must be scheduled and called at least 45 calendar days in advance, to be held explicitly within a General Assembly —ordinary or extraordinary— with the mandatory inclusion of the elections in the agenda.

5. When there is an electoral process, whether for the election of the entire Board of Trustees or partially (as provided in Article 11.3), it shall be the first item on the agenda after welcoming attendees, and no decision or vote may be taken before said election.

6. All members of the Association have the right to run as candidates.

7. Candidacies must be submitted with sufficient advance notice as determined before the elections, to ensure that the Nobodies Board of Trustees can communicate and disclose said candidacies to all members.

8. The Board of Trustees serves without any remuneration.

### Article 12. Term of Office and Termination of Board Members.

1. Members of the Board of Trustees serve for a period of two years and may be re-elected consecutively for a maximum of four terms, amounting to a total of eight consecutive years. Upon reaching this limit, they must step down and may not be elected again to any position within the Board of Trustees until at least one full term has elapsed.

2. The termination of positions before the end of their term may occur for:

   a. Death or declaration of absence in the case of natural persons, or dissolution in the case of legal persons.

   b. Incapacity or disqualification for professional practice, as well as any final judicial conviction resulting in loss of active or passive suffrage capacity for any of the reasons set forth in the Criminal Procedure Act.

   c. Resignation notified to the governing body.

   d. Removal agreed by the General Assembly.

   e. Any other cause established by law or by these Statutes.

3. Vacancies arising in the Board of Trustees shall be filled in accordance with the provisions set out above (Article 11.2).

### Article 13. Powers of the Board of Trustees.

The Board of Trustees has the following powers:

1. To represent, direct and manage Nobodies in the broadest manner recognized by law; likewise, to implement the decisions adopted by the General Assembly, in accordance with the rules, instructions and guidelines established by said Assembly.

2. To adopt the necessary resolutions regarding appearances before public bodies and to exercise all types of legal actions and lodge the relevant appeals.

3. To propose to the General Assembly any actions it intends to carry out in defence of the interests and in pursuit of the objectives of Nobodies.

4. To propose to the General Assembly any relevant amendments or requirements regarding the fees to be paid by the members of the Association.

5. To convene the General Assemblies —ordinary and, where applicable, extraordinary— and ensure that the resolutions adopted are implemented.

6. To submit the balance sheet and financial statements for each financial year to the General Assembly for approval, and to draw up the budget for the following financial year.

7. To hire any employees necessary for the achievement of its founding objectives.

8. To establish and coordinate working groups in order to achieve the purposes of Nobodies in the most efficient and effective way, and, where required, to authorize the actions that these groups intend to carry out.

9. To appoint, at the proposal of the working groups themselves, the members of the Association who shall be in charge of each working group.

10. To open current accounts and savings accounts in any credit or savings institution and to dispose of the funds deposited therein. The use of these funds is determined in Article 28.

11. To provisionally resolve any matter not foreseen in the Statutes and report on it at the immediately following meeting of the General Assembly that is convened.

12. Any other power not specifically attributed to another governing body of the Association, or expressly delegated to it.

### Article 14. Meetings of the Board of Trustees.

1. The Board of Trustees, convened in advance by the President or by the person replacing them, must meet in ordinary session as often as its members decide, which in any case may not be less than twice a year.

2. It shall meet in extraordinary session when convened as such by the President, or if requested by one third of its members.

### Article 15. Formation and Adoption of Resolutions of the Board of Trustees.

1. The Board of Trustees shall be validly constituted if it has been duly convened and there is a quorum of half plus one of its members.

2. The members of the Board of Trustees are obliged to attend all meetings that are convened, although they may excuse themselves for justified reasons. The attendance of the President or the Secretary, or, where applicable, of the persons replacing them by prior delegation, is always required.

3. The Board of Trustees adopts its resolutions by simple majority of the votes of those present. In the event of a tie, the vote of the President of the Board of Trustees, or of the person designated by them, shall be deemed a casting vote with tie-breaking effect.

### Article 16. Delegation of Powers and Appointment of Representatives.

1. The Board of Trustees may delegate some of its powers to one or more committees or working groups if it has the favourable vote of two-thirds of its members.

2. It may also appoint, with the same quorum, one or more representatives to perform the tasks entrusted to them with the powers it deems appropriate to grant in each case.

### Article 17. Minutes and Record of Agreements of the Board of Trustees.

1. The resolutions of the Board of Trustees must be recorded in the minutes and signed by the Secretary and the President. At the beginning of each meeting of the Board of Trustees, the minutes of the previous session must be read so that they may be approved or, where appropriate, amended.

2. The minutes shall be fully and immediately accessible to the members of the Board, always in compliance with data protection regulations.

### Article 18. Duties of the Members of the Board of Trustees.

1. The President shall have the following duties:

   a. To direct and legally represent Nobodies, by delegation of the General Assembly and the Board of Trustees.

   b. To chair the meetings of the General Assembly and the Board of Trustees, and to delegate such chairmanship, in duly justified and exceptional cases where attendance must be excused, to another member of the Board of Trustees.

      In the General Assembly, the conduct or moderation of the debates shall be carried out by the person designated by the Assembly at the beginning of each session, who may be any member of the Assembly.

      In the Board of Trustees, the President shall chair the debates, without prejudice to the possibility of delegating this function to another member of the Board of Trustees when deemed appropriate.

   c. To cast a decisive casting vote in the event of a tie.

   d. To convene the meetings of the General Assembly and of the Board of Trustees.

   e. To endorse the minutes and certificates drawn up by the Secretary of the Association.

   f. Any other duties inherent to the position and those delegated by the General Assembly or the Board of Trustees.

2. The duties of the Vice President are:

   a. To replace the President in cases of absence or illness.

3. The Treasurer has the following duties:

   a. The custody and control of the Association's financial resources.

   b. The preparation of the budget, balance sheet and settlement of accounts.

   c. The supervision of the custody and control of financial resources, and the oversight of the preparation of the budget and accounting books.

   d. Signing fee receipts and other treasury documents.

   e. Creating and maintaining a "Large Expense Policy" that defines the thresholds and authorization workflows for significant expenditures. This policy must be presented and published alongside the annual budget for transparency.

   f. Once a year, the Treasurer shall present the financial statements for the year and the budget for the following year at an ordinary —or, where applicable, extraordinary— meeting of the General Assembly of the members of the Association.

4. The Secretary must:

   a. Keep custody of the documentation of the Association.

   b. Draw up and record the minutes of the meetings of the General Assembly and the Board of Trustees.

   c. Draft and authorize the certificates to be issued to third parties.

   d. Keep the register of member entities.

   e. Exercise legal representation before the relevant authorities (Register of Associations) regarding changes that occur both in the Board of Trustees and in the General Assembly, as well as any amendments to the Statutes or dissolution of the Association that may take place.

5. The Vocales shall have the powers inherent to their status as members of the Board of Trustees. They shall have the right to speak and vote in all sessions, participating in the deliberations and decision-making of the Board, as well as in the commissions or working groups assigned to them.

### Article 19. Policy for Guests

The Board of Trustees may invite to its meetings persons whose presence it considers of interest, who shall participate with a voice but without a vote. Likewise, it may create advisory bodies or committees formed by members or external experts to collaborate on specific aims of the Association.

## CHAPTER III. COMMITTEES OR WORKING GROUPS

### Article 20. Supervision of Committees and Working Groups.

1. The Association shall oversee the creation of committees or working groups that carry out support tasks of various kinds for it.

2. The Board of Trustees shall have supervisory functions in order to analyse the different working groups, whose heads must submit to it once a year a detailed report of their activities.

### Article 21. Admission of Members.

1. All persons who so wish, who are of legal age and have full legal capacity, whose application is approved by the Board of Trustees, may become part of Nobodies.

2. In order to join the Association, it is necessary to submit a written application to the Board of Trustees, which will inform the interested person of the steps to be taken, the required documentation and the different categories of members. Once the application has been submitted, the Board of Trustees must respond within a maximum period of 30 days.

3. Membership registration shall have a maximum duration of two years, expiring on December 31st of the second year. Membership shall be renewable at the request of the member and with the authorization of the Board of Trustees, without limit.

### Article 22. Rights of Members.

The members of the Association shall have the following rights:

1. To attend meetings of the General Assembly with the right to speak and vote, being able to exercise these rights both in person and by telematic or electronic means.

2. To elect and be elected for positions on the Board of Trustees.

3. To exercise the representation conferred on them in each case, with the inherent powers thereof, as well as those assigned by the Board of Trustees, where applicable.

4. To take part in the governance, management, services and activities of the Association, in accordance with legal and statutory rules.

5. To have full access, if so requested from the Board of Trustees, to the treasury and accounting reports.

6. To present to the Assembly and to the Board of Trustees any matters they consider may contribute to enriching the life of Nobodies and to making the achievement of its basic social objectives more effective.

7. To request and obtain explanations regarding the administration and management of the Board of Trustees.

8. To be heard prior to the adoption of disciplinary measures.

9. To receive information on the activities and progress of the work of the Association.

10. To be able to take part in working groups after the approval of the Board of Trustees.

11. To endorse and confirm, or otherwise oppose and veto, according to the criteria expressed by a simple majority of all members gathered in General Assembly, the decisions of the Board of Trustees.

12. To challenge the resolutions of the bodies of the Association that they deem contrary to the Law or to the Statutes.

### Article 23. Duties of Members.

All members shall have the following duties:

1. To commit to the objectives of Nobodies and actively participate in their achievement.

2. To comply with these Statutes.

3. To comply with the resolutions of the General Assemblies, both ordinary and extraordinary, and with the rules laid down by the Board of Trustees and the governing bodies for their implementation, within the framework of the Association's regulations.

4. To contribute to the support and maintenance of the Association by paying fees, surcharges and other financial contributions established by the Statutes and approved in accordance with them.

5. To fulfil the rest of the obligations arising from the statutory provisions.

6. Without prejudice to the provisions of Organic Law 1/2002, of 22 March, regulating the Right of Association, and Law 19/2013, of 9 December, on transparency, access to public information and good governance, members shall uphold discretion and confidentiality with regard to public information on decisions and deliberations of the General Assembly and the Board of Trustees of the Association.

### Article 24. Collaborators of the Association.

Without technically having the status of members, a category of collaborators is established for those persons or entities that, wishing to contribute to the maintenance and development of the Association and the promotion of its purposes, are accepted by the Board of Trustees, which shall determine the minimum amount of financial contribution, which in no case shall exceed one quarter of the membership fee. Application shall be directed to the Board of Trustees, alongside the required data, and the Board shall decide on it in a period no longer than thirty days.

### Article 25. Rights of Collaborators.

Collaborators shall have the following rights:

1. To take part in the activities of the Association and receive all its information and publications.

2. To participate with voice but without vote in the General Assemblies.

3. To have full access, if so requested from the Board of Trustees, to the treasury and accounting reports.

4. To be informed of the composition of the governing and representative bodies of the Association and of the development of its activities.

5. To be heard prior to the adoption of disciplinary measures.

6. To challenge the resolutions of the bodies of the Association that they deem contrary to the Law or to the Statutes.

### Article 26. Grounds for Removal of Members and Collaborators.

1. The following are grounds for removal as a member or collaborator of Nobodies:

   a. By decision of the member, who must notify the Board of Trustees in writing, at least one calendar month prior to the date of termination.

   b. Failure to pay the established fees (ordinary or extraordinary) for a period of 2 years.

   c. Failure to comply with statutory obligations.

   d. When the member or collaborator attempts to use Nobodies or their status as a member for purposes not set out in Article 2.

2. When termination is due to the reasons mentioned in points 3 and 4 of this article, it shall be mandatory to open a file by decision of the Board of Trustees; the person concerned must be heard if they so request. The final decision on termination shall be taken by the General Assembly at the proposal of the Board of Trustees, and shall only be ratified by a resolution of said Assembly, by a qualified majority of 2/3 of the members present.

## CHAPTER V. FINANCIAL REGIME AND DOCUMENTATION

### Article 27. Accounting, Inventory, and Minute Book.

The Association shall keep an up-to-date list of its members. It shall also keep accounts that reflect a true and fair view of the assets, results, financial situation of the entity and the activities carried out. It shall also keep an up-to-date inventory of its assets.

A Minute Book shall contain the minutes of the meetings held by its governing and representative bodies.

### Article 28. Financial resources of the Association.

The financial resources of the Association are obtained from:

a. The fees set by the General Assembly for its members.

b. Any public or private subsidies obtained.

c. Donations, inheritances or legacies received.

d. Other income that may be obtained through training, cultural or any other type of activities carried out.

### Article 29. Financial Obligations of Members.

1. All members of Nobodies are obliged to support it financially, jointly and proportionally, through ordinary fees or extraordinary contributions, in the manner and proportion determined by the General Assembly at the proposal of the Board of Trustees.

2. The Association shall establish an annual membership fee, the payment of which shall be a requirement to acquire and maintain the status of member, with the rights and responsibilities entailed in accordance with the Statutes and resolutions of the entity. This fee is intended to contribute to the general support and long-term common objectives of the Association.

3. The Association may define specific forms of financial contributions linked to participation in activities, initiatives or specific projects that it organizes. These contributions shall entitle participation in each of the activities and must be approved by the General Assembly, depending on the costs involved and in accordance with criteria of economic accessibility, to ensure the participation of people with different levels of resources.

4. No contribution, whether larger or symbolic, shall grant preferential rights or imply any hierarchical position within the Association, but shall be understood as a free act of commitment to it, in line with the fundamental principles that govern it.

### Article 30. Financial year and Approval of Accounts.

1. The financial year shall coincide with the calendar year, therefore starting on 1 January and ending on 31 December of each year.

2. Each year the Board of Trustees shall draw up the Budget, which shall be approved by the General Assembly. With the approval of said Budget, the ordinary fees for the corresponding financial year shall be deemed approved.

3. For the approval of extraordinary fees, an Extraordinary General Assembly must be convened, unless the Association lacks liquidity and the corresponding expenditure is urgent, in which case the resolution of the Board of Trustees shall suffice, following a report by the Treasurer and subsequent ratification by the General Assembly, which must take place within thirty days following the resolution of the Board of Trustees.

4. The General Assembly shall annually approve the accounts of the Association, once the financial year to which they correspond has ended.

5. The Board of Trustees shall keep the corresponding accounting books, which shall provide a true and fair view of the assets, results and financial situation of the Association.

### Article 31. Bank Accounts and Authorized Signatures.

The signatures of the President, the Treasurer and the Secretary must appear on current accounts opened in banking institutions, and may be extended to other natural or legal persons if deemed necessary, subject to ratification by the General Assembly of such increase in authorized signatories.

The disposal of funds shall be governed by the Large Expense Policy established by the Board and the Treasurer. This policy will determine the specific limits and requirements for individual vs. joint authorizations to ensure both financial security and operational agility.

## CHAPTER VI. DISCIPLINARY REGIME

### Article 32. Disciplinary Regime.

1. The Board of Trustees may sanction offences committed by members.

2. These offences may be classified as minor, serious or very serious, and the corresponding sanctions may range from a warning to expulsion from the Association, a decision that must be ratified by the General Assembly (Article 8).

3. The disciplinary procedure shall be initiated ex officio or as a result of a complaint or communication submitted by any member, which may be anonymous, but needs to be motivated. Within 10 days, the Board of Trustees shall appoint a person belonging to the Association to handle the disciplinary file. The member subject to the disciplinary procedure shall be summoned to present their arguments, as shall the person who submitted the request for sanctions, within the following twenty-one calendar days, and a decision on the file shall be issued within no more than 45 days from the appointment of the person responsible for the file.

---

## CHAPTER VII. DISSOLUTION OF THE ASSOCIATION

### Article 33. Dissolution of the Association.

The Association shall be voluntarily dissolved when so resolved by the Extraordinary General Assembly, convened for this purpose and in accordance with the provisions of these Statutes, with a majority of two thirds of the votes.

Likewise, the Association shall be dissolved for the following reasons:

a. Due to the impossibility of fulfilling the purposes set out in the Statutes, as determined by resolution of the General Assembly.

b. By court order.

### Article 34. Liquidation and Allocation of Assets.

In the event of dissolution, a liquidation committee shall be appointed to settle the Association's debts and liquidate its assets. Any remaining net assets shall be allocated to a public entity or a private non-profit entity pursuing purposes of general interest similar to those of this Association, in a manner that preserves its non-profit nature, in accordance with the provisions of Organic Law 1/2002.

The liquidators shall have the functions established in sections 3 and 4 of Article 18 of Organic Law 1/2002 of 22 March.

---

## CHAPTER VIII. DATA PROTECTION.

### Article 35. Data Protection

The Association guarantees compliance with applicable personal data protection regulations, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Spanish Organic Law 3/2018 on Personal Data Protection and the Guarantee of Digital Rights (LOPD-GDD).

Personal data of members shall be processed exclusively for the purpose of managing their relationship with the Association. At the time of application for membership, new members shall be informed about the processing of their data and their express consent shall be obtained.

Members may exercise, at any time, their rights of access, rectification, erasure, objection, restriction of processing, and data portability by contacting the Association's Board of Trustees.

---

## ADDITIONAL PROVISION

In all matters not provided for in these Statutes, the current Organic Law 1/2002, of 22 March, regulating the Right of Association, and its supplementary provisions shall apply.
